TRAVEL TIPS

  • It's easy to survive in Korea using English alone, but you'll have more fun, eat better, and make more friends if you learn a few phrases. See the language section above ;)
  • Bring a credit card to Korea (check intl. fees) and KNOW YOUR PIN! While you might use cash for street food and souveniers, most other places will take card and convert your money to Korean Won automatically. If you know your pin number, you can use your credit card to withrawal money from Korean ATMs. Using your credit card will likely get you a better exchange rate than exchanging at the airport or elsewhere.
  • Before you get on a train from Incheon Airport to Seoul, stop by a T-Money machine and buy a transit card and put at least ₩10,000 (~$10) on it so that you can easily bop around Seoul. Buses and many taxis also use this transit card as payment.
  • If you don't have free or cheap international data on your cell phone plan, consider getting a Korean sim card at the airport. It's cheap, but your phone needs to be unlocked. Ask your mobile carrier to unlock your phone before the trip (beware, you might have to pay off a financed phone to get it unlocked).
  • When you arrive at your hotel, ask the front desk to write your address down in Korean so that you can show taxi drivers taking you home. Some hotels will already have cards like this printed for you to take with you.
  • When visiting Korea, one tends to drink more than one's used to. Drink a lot of water (bottled water is cheap here!) and bring ibuprofin.
  • Download a Korean Subway system app to help you get around Seoul efficiently. (iOS, iOS) (Android)
  • Download KakaoTalk, Korea's most popular chat app. When you make a friend, share IDs! (iOS) (Android)
  • Familiarize yourself with the Naver ("Korea's Google") Maps app. It works much better than Google Maps in Korea. (iOS) (Android)
